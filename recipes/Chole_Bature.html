Chole Bature

Description

"Chole Bature" is a popular North Indian dish consisting of two main components: "Chole" (spiced chickpea curry) and "Bature" (deep-fried bread). It's a delicious and hearty dish that's often enjoyed as a special treat or on festive occasions. Here's a breakdown of the ingredients and how to make Chole Bhature:

Ingredients

Chole

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cups canned chickpeas)
  • Water for soaking (if using dried chickpeas)
  • 1 tea bag (optional, for color)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda (if using dried chickpeas)
  • Salt to taste

Chole Masala

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala

Bature

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • oil

Instructions

For Preparing Chole

  1. Wash and soak dried chickpeas in plenty of water overnight. In the morning, drain and rinse them.
  2. In a large pot, add the soaked chickpeas, enough water to cover them, a tea bag (for color), and a pinch of baking soda. Boil until the chickpeas are tender. This may take about 20-30 minutes. Drain and discard the tea bag.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add minced garlic and green chilies and sauté for a minute. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  4. Add turmeric, coriander powder, cumin powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook the masala until it's well roasted and aromatic.
  5. Add the cooked chickpeas to the masala and mix well. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. If needed, add water to achieve your desired consistency.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve with lemon wedges.

For Bature

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, yogurt, baking powder, baking soda, salt, sugar, and vegetable oil. Mix well. Add water gradually to make a soft, pliable dough. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes.
  2. Cover the dough and let it rest for 2-3 hours at room temperature.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into thin discs. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry the rolled dough one at a time until they puff up and turn golden brown on both sides. Drain on paper towels.
